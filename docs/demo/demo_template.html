Bayesian

Turn the prior-tightness dial

The Minnesota prior shrinks a VAR toward random walks. Tight (λ = 0.05) means smooth, confident, possibly-too-simple responses; loose (λ = 1.0) approaches the noisy OLS answer. The log marginal likelihood — computable in closed form for this conjugate family — says which the evidence prefers. Responses of all three variables to a GDP-growth shock, with 90% credible bands from 600 posterior draws.

Volatility

Volatility clusters — and GARCH forecasts it

Returns are nearly unpredictable; their volatility is not. A GARCH(1,1) tracks the calm and stormy regimes and its forecast mean-reverts to the long-run level. Switch the innovation distribution and compare fits — fat tails change the likelihood, barely move the variance path.

Robust volatility

Score-driven volatility shrugs off the jumps GARCH chases

A GAS (generalized autoregressive score) model drives the variance by the score of the observation density. With a Student-t density that score soft-thresholds outliers, so an isolated jump (dashed lines) barely moves the estimate — while the Gaussian score reacts one-for-one. Toggle the densities and watch the jumps.

Density
tsecon.gas_volatility · GAS(1,1) score-driven variance (Creal-Koopman-Lucas 2013) · dashed verticals mark isolated jumps · the t-score down-weights each observation by 1/(1 + y²/((ν−2)f))

Local projections, one regression per horizon

Instead of iterating a VAR, run a separate regression at each horizon — robust to misspecification of the far dynamics. The band is honest 90% inference; lag augmentation (Montiel Olea-Plagborg-Møller) is the default, and plain Newey-West HAC is a click away. Both track the true response (dashed).

High frequency

Splitting realized variance into diffusion and jumps

With intraday data you can measure a day's volatility almost exactly — and split it. Bipower variation is jump-robust, so realized-variance-minus-bipower isolates the jump contribution, and the BNS ratio test flags which days actually jumped (markers).

Series
tsecon.realized_measures / tsecon.bns_jump_test · realized variance vs jump-robust bipower · markers: days the BNS ratio test flags as jumps (BNS 2004; Huang-Tauchen 2005)
